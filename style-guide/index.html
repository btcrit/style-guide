Style Guide?

A style guide helps create a consistent Brand Image. Our website, posters, flyers, handouts, etc. should all represent BTCRIT in a predictable manner.

That being said, there is no rule saying to use this. To be honest, I built this purely to help the design process for WebsiteV2. If our poster designers and others choose to follow this, I'll consider it a bonus.

Brand Ethos

Club Purpose

Decentralized Systems: Education and Implementation

In public media, we should strive to direct people towards our learning resources, such as our website, meetings, or contact information of willing club members.

Club Audience

College students at RIT.

Our target audience requires a specific tone of voice to have the maximum impact. For more information, see the Tone and Voice section.

Logo/Brandname

Logo

Currently, for now, temporarily, for the time being, our logo is the BTCRIT Hamster.

The logo is 200px by 200px, though due to its SVG nature, it can be scaled to any size. Below 64px, detail is lost - at small sizes, a square of accent color is preffered. Around the Logo, at least 1rem of space is preffered.

Appropriate backgrounds include Dark, DarkGray, and Base. Accent and lighter colors provide too little contrast to be suitable. See the Color Section for more information.

Brandname

Blockchain Technology Club

  • Stand-Alone, no other words should follow it.
  • Roboto Bold

Short Form:

BTCRIT

Color Palette

Dark: #1e1e1e

DarkGray: #43494D

LightGray: #E9EBED

Light: #FBFBFB

Base: #10668B

Accent: #DD7C10

The main color of the logo was extracted as the Accent color. From there, Base was chosen to complement it.

Dark and Light are standard colors, whereas darkgray and lightgray are modified to subtly match the Base.

In general, Base and the Grayscale variants should be the most common. The less frequently accent is used, the more powerful it will be.

On this page, the background is Light. Text is Dark. Links are Base, but turn to Accent on Hover.

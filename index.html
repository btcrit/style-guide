Style Guide?

A style guide helps create a consistent Brand Image. Our website, posters, flyers, handouts, etc. should all represent BTCRIT in a predictable manner.

That being said, there is no rule saying to use this. To be honest, I built this purely to help the design process for WebsiteV2. If our poster designers and others choose to follow this, I'll consider it a bonus.

Logo, Brandname

Logo

Currently, for now, temporarily, for the time being, our logo is the BTCRIT Hamster.

The logo is 200px by 200px, though due to its SVG nature, it can be scaled to any size. Below 64px, detail is lost - at small sizes, a square of accent color is preffered. Around the Logo, at least 1rem of space is preffered.

Appropriate backgrounds include Dark, DarkGray, and Base. Accent and lighter colors provide too little contrast to be suitable. See the Color Section for more information.

Color Palette

Dark: #1e1e1e

DarkGray: #43494D

Gray: #E9EBED

LightGray: #F5F5F5

Light: #FBFBFB

Base: #10668B

Accent: #DD7C10

The main color of the logo was extracted as the Accent color. From there, Base was chosen such that Accent would complement it.

Dark and Light are standard colors. When Base is overlayed on Dark and Light with low opacity, the result is Darkgray and Gray. Lightgray is a slightly darker Light, useful for backgrounds.

Base is a general root color, from which all others arise.

Light is very close to white. Light causes items to 'pop' off of a LightGray background.

LightGray is very neutral, and is ideal for large backgrounds.

Gray has influences from Base, and is good for highlighting small sections, or sinking an area away from LightGray.

DarkGray is just kinda here, I don't really have a specific use for it.

Dark is my favorite shade of gray. It's roughly off-black, making it a suitable background and font color.

Accent is more powerful the less it is used. Accent is best used on link hover events and borders on important things.

Typography

Roboto 700: Impactful, Stand-Out

For block text, Open Sans 400 was chosen. Serif fonts have lots of small details distinguishing letters. In print, these small details make reading easier. On screens however, serifs are lost into anti-aliasing noise, making Sans-Serif fonts appear cleaner and easier to read.

Also available:

Open Sans 400i - same as above, but italics.

Roboto 400, more impactful than Open Sans, without the bold.

Use of space

All elements should be separated by at least 1rem. This should be accomplished via Margins, though additional spacing with padding is appropriate in some situations.

Use overflow: auto to prevent margins from overflowing the parent without adding unnesesary padding.

1rem margins should be everywhere. Items in boxes should also employ 1rem padding.
